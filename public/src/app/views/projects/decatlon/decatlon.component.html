All along my 5 years of study we had projects in partnership with companies, these kind of projects were a great way for us to test our ideas with a real business model in mind. This project was a partnership with Oxylan Décatlon, a French sportswear company with subsidiaries in China and Australia. During a week of workshop we lead a project around « The ideal sport experience who could motivate & attract Chinese non-enthusiast to sport ».

We started this project with a series of interview with students of Shanghai University, from their feedback and our research, we realise than in China sport was more about preventing disease and not having a good body how it would be the case in a Western Country. We dig this idea and found that sport is a really good way to cure depression and in China depression touch a large part of population. We decide to use the emergent iot technology to develop a connected wrist. After few iteration we agree on a shape and a user experience with the ideas to push the them to enjoy a moment of quick casual sport through daily challenges.

We never had as objective a fully functional project, instead we were looking for a proof of concept, producing a sufficient amount a content and support to confirm ours hypothesis. A kakemono poster was produced in order to summarize these objective and hypothesis. Few 3D modelizations let us show our first vision for a solution. An I did develop a prototype for the mobile application, not to much about the final user interface but about the final user experience as a compagnon of the wrist.
